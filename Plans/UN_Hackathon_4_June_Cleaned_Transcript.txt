Context from the workshop materials

The knowledge workshop focused on legal structure, data-collection challenges, end-user needs, and engineering lessons for building AI tools for digital trade regulatory analysis. Professor Henry Gao’s slides explained how domestic statutes are typically structured: preamble/introduction, definitions, substantive obligations, institutional provisions, enforcement/penalties, judicial review, appendices, and cross-references or implementing regulations. The EUI presentation emphasized that useful outputs should become database-ready structured findings with auditable citations, original text, translations, timelines, confidence flags, and reviewer decisions. The WTO end-user presentation stressed methodology-first thinking, clear benchmarks, the universe of applicable laws, exceptions and implementation, accurate law identification, translation of legal terminology, and source accuracy.
